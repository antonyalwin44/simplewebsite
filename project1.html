Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a minaret and victory tower in Delhi. It is the tallest minaret in India and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The construction was started in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi. It served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. The fort's massive red sandstone walls, which stand 75 feet high, were built in 1638 to keep out invaders.

Hampi

Hampi is an ancient village in the northern part of Karnataka, India. It was one of the richest and largest cities in the world during its prime. The ruins of Hampi, as it is known today, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
